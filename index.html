Frequently Asked Questions

What is GST and how is it calculated? ▼

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a unified indirect tax in India. It replaced multiple taxes like VAT, Service Tax, and Excise Duty. GST is calculated as: GST Amount = (Original Price × GST Rate) / 100. For example, on ₹10,000 at 18%, the GST is ₹1,800 making the total ₹11,800.

What is the difference between CGST, SGST, and IGST? ▼

CGST (Central GST) and SGST (State GST) are both charged on intra-state (within same state) transactions, each at half the total GST rate. IGST (Integrated GST) is charged on inter-state transactions (between different states) at the full GST rate and is collected by the Central Government.

What is GST inclusive vs exclusive? ▼

GST Exclusive means GST is added on top of the base price. GST Inclusive means the price already includes GST and you need to extract the tax component. Use our "Remove GST" tab to reverse-calculate GST from an inclusive price.

Which items are exempt from GST in India? ▼

Items exempt from GST (0% slab) include fresh fruits and vegetables, milk, eggs, meat, fish, bread, salt, and educational services. Alcohol, petrol, diesel, and electricity are outside the GST regime and are taxed separately by state governments.

What is the GST rate on services in India for FY 2025-26? ▼

For FY 2025-26, most services in India continue to attract 18% GST. This includes IT services, banking, insurance, telecom, and professional services. Restaurants typically charge 5% GST. Healthcare and educational services remain exempt. Hotels above ₹7,500 per night attract 18% GST. The GST Council has proposed reduced rates on life and health insurance premiums in FY 2025-26 — consult your insurer for the latest applicable rate.

What are the key GST changes for FY 2025-26? ▼

For Financial Year 2025-26, key GST updates include: the four-tier rate structure (5%, 12%, 18%, 28%) remains unchanged; e-invoicing is mandatory for businesses with turnover above ₹5 crore; the GST annual return (GSTR-9) filing threshold continues at ₹2 crore; and the GST Council is working on rate rationalisation including possible reduction on insurance premiums and certain goods. Always verify current rates with a chartered accountant or the official GST portal at gstn.org.in.
